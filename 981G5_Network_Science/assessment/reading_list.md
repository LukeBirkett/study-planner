## 3. [A multilayer network framework for soccer analysis (Novillo et al., 2024)]()

In their 2024 paper "A multilayer network framework for soccer analysis," Álvaro Novillo, Javier M. Buldú, and colleagues still use passes as the primary interaction/edge, but they divide them into different types across multiple layers. Instead of aggregating all passes into a single network layer, their multilayer framework separates the game's passing data to capture tactical nuance. The layers in this framework are defined by separating passes based on specific sub-categories or contexts, such as:
- **Types of passes:** Separating short passes vs. long passes, or ground passes vs. aerial passes.
- **Spatial/Directional orientation:** Categorizing progressive/forward passes, lateral passes, and backward passes into separate layers.
- **Pitch Zones:** Breaking down passes depending on the zone of the pitch they occur in (e.g., build-up in the defensive third vs. creation in the attacking third).

> Novillo, A., Gong, B., Martinez, J. H., Resta, R., del Campo, R. L. L., and Buldu, J. M.(2024). A multilayer network framework for soccer analysis. Chaos Soliton. Fract. 178.doi: 10.1016/j.chaos.2023.114355

## 4. [A network theory analysis of football strategies (Pena and Touchette, 2012)]()

- Seminal/Early paper demonstating applying networks to football
- "We showcase in this paper the use of some tools from network theory to describe the strategy of football teams"
- construct for each team a weighted and directed network in which nodes correspond to players and arrows to passes (Peña and Touchette, 2012). 
- Using different centrality measures, we can also determine the relative importance of each player in the game, the `popularity' of a player, and the effect of removing players from the game (Peña and Touchette, 2012).

> Peña, J. L., and Touchette, H. (2012). A network theory analysis of football strategies. in Proc. 2012 Euromech Physics of Sports Conference, pages 517–528

## 6. [On the use of passing network indicators to predict football outcomes (Ievoli et al., 2021)]()

- Take networks and their properties for **prediction** task
- Passing networks and their structural features can be used to evaluate the style of play in terms of passing behavior, analyzing and quantifying interactions among players.
- directly compute and discuss network properties, such as centralization, clustering and cliques, from a football perspective.
- some network-based variables, such as diameter and betweenness centralization, can be related to the level of offensive actions and finalizations for a team.

> Ievoli, R., Palazzo, L., and Ragozini, G. (2021). On the use of passing network indicators to predict football outcomes.
