#### Quorum
In the primary analytical Markov model of Robinson et al. (2011), the quorum switch is intentionally abstracted or skipped.

The Markov matrix ($Q$) focuses entirely on the first phase of the decision: individual commitment to recruitment (the individual threshold check).

The seminar slides reference Stephen Pratt’s work (2005) to explain the biological mechanism: Quorum sensing occurs via localized encounters with nestmates.

When a scout ant is inside a new nest site, she does not have global knowledge of how many total ants are there. 

Instead, she relies on a local proxy: encounter rate. As more ants are recruited to a site via tandem runs, the physical density of ants in that cavity increases.

The scout continues walking around the cavity. Every time she physically bumps into another nestmate, it triggers a sensory cue. If the frequency of these physical collisions exceeds a specific threshold number within a certain timeframe, the ant "senses" a quorum and switches her behavior from leading tandem runs to physically picking up and carrying passive colony members.
